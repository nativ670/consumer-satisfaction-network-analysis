The Cognitive Network of Experience:
Mapping Consumer Satisfaction as an Emergent Property

Nativ, Hebrew University of Jerusalem
Introduction
  • The Multi-Attribute Problem: Traditional models assume satisfaction is a simple sum of independent feature weights3.
  • Cognitive Interdependence: The Halo Effect & Spreading Activation theory tell us that consumers evaluate attributes interactively using compensatory heuristics6,2.
  • Network Paradigm: Reconceptualizing satisfaction as an emergent property of a dynamic interacting attribute network1.
  • The J-Shaped Paradox: Review distributions indicate that consumer evaluation reflects polarized behavioral groups rather than a continuous state4.
Research Objectives

Theoretical Question

"How are experience attributes represented and weighted in individuos' mental models? Do they rely on isolated features or complex behavioral schemas driven by interactions?"

Operational Question

"Does incorporating measured network interactions into a predictive model improve the accuracy of forecasting overall customer satisfaction compared to traditional models?"

The Attribute Interaction Network

Network Structure: Two distinct clusters emerged: External/Logistical (Service, Price, Packaging) and Internal/Sensory (Ingredients, Smell, Texture). The system is bridged structurally by Packaging and Ingredients.

Key Finding #1

Most interaction coefficients were negative, confirming mathematical trade-offs.

Key Finding #2

The ordinal model predominantly predicted extreme ratings (1★ and 5★).

Discussion & Synthesis

Satisfaction emerges from conditional interactions between attributes rather than independent contributions — consistent with Gestalt evaluation and Spreading Activation theory5,2.

Predominantly negative interactions suggest compensatory trade-offs, where strengths in one aspect (e.g., Sensory) can offset weaknesses in another (e.g., Logistical).

While predictive gains were modest, the network substantially improved model fit (BIC), suggesting its primary contribution is explanatory rather than purely predictive.
